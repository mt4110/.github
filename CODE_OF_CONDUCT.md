# Code of Conduct

This project aims to be a calm, respectful place to collaborate.

This Code of Conduct applies to issues, pull requests, code review, discussions, and other public spaces connected to the project.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that help the project:

- be respectful, patient, and specific
- focus feedback on the code, docs, or behavior rather than the person
- share context, tradeoffs, and reproduction steps clearly
- welcome contributors with different backgrounds and experience levels
- accept correction gracefully and help others do the same

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- harassment, discrimination, intimidation, or personal attacks
- sexualized language or unwanted attention
- trolling, baiting, dogpiling, or sustained disruption
- publishing someone else's private information without permission
- repeated bad-faith misrepresentation of another person's work or intent

## Scope

This policy applies in project spaces and in public spaces when someone is acting as a maintainer or contributor for the project.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, contact the maintainers privately through GitHub.

For security-related concerns, follow [SECURITY.md](./SECURITY.md) instead of opening a public issue.

When practical, include links, screenshots, or a short timeline so the report can be reviewed quickly and fairly.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit or remove comments, close conversations, reject contributions, or temporarily or permanently limit participation when behavior harms the project or its contributors.

Responses depend on the severity and pattern of the behavior.

Retaliation against people who make good-faith reports is not acceptable.

## Shared Expectation

Leave the collaboration calmer, clearer, and more useful than you found it.
